---
title: "Modal thinking - Separate decision making and execution"
slug: "modal-thinking"
---

Decision Mode:
- Empirical: what decisions (avoidance included) made you regret before.
- An arbitrary decision is still hundredfold better than an impulsive driven one.

Execution Mode:
- You have no other option.
- You don’t stop in the middle
- Whether it’s a great or bad move is up to be judge by the Decision Mode’s reflection.
- Even it turns out to be a bad decision, the outcome of strengthen your adherence, self-worth, and trust in the heuristic is long term yield.

This way, you saved it tons of mental energy from bounced thinking.
