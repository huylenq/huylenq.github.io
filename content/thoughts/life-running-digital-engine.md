Comprehensively composed of all the sub-systems for how I run most aspects of my life where a rule-engine system is employable.

This system's overall implementation strongly prefers using apps. The reason being to leverage app **notifications** along with the <span class="private-link">First-screen Principle</span> to alleviate the mental tax of decision making and the constant anxiety of “doing the right thing”.

Above all of the sub-systems is the mindset that govern their execution. The current model to adhere to is <span class="private-link">States of Mind</span>.

### Driver Todo System

You take the driver seat.

App: **Things**

Every task and project is an opportunity to pick up. They have a *positive* desirable outcome to pursue. Every progress pushes your life forward.

The performance is not preallocated, not time-scheduled. The **mentality** when executing the task backlog when you are in the driver sea is with high urgency: "could I done this with the minimal time?", or "could I done the best job for this in 30 minutes?". *The faster I accomplish the outcome, the better*.

Excluded of routine, scheduled tasks. This goal-todo system contains opportunities, not obligations (there's a YouTube video from Ali Abdaal regarding this).

Related: <span class="private-link">Healthy todo backlog</span>

### Accountability Todo System

Non-negotiable, execute with no wasted mental cycle, that is the beauty of the system.

Apps: **Calendar** + **Reminders** + **Streaks**

The system manage *obligations* and *routines* that I just mindlessly do. Most of them are time-scheduled or have a very clear do-able context.

The "**when to do it**" rule:
- When it is due, evening for date-scheduled, when it appears for datetime-scheduled.
- In context (location, equipment). There's only a single context now:
    - *Groceries*: when I'm at a grocery store.

### Reflection Cadence

Reflection cadence is the system **dynamic** of evolution of self-stabilizing.  Reflection sessions are performed routinely, on recurrent schedules.

There are three cycles, **daily**, **weekly** and **monthly**. They are scheduled with noted details within the Reminders app:

- <span class="private-link">The Day</span>
- <span class="private-link">Weekly Reflection</span>
- <span class="private-link">Monthly Reflection</span>
